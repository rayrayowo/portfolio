Data Science x Financial Technology

Building clear, useful stories from data.

I am Ruiyang (Ray) Zhang, a Northeastern University data science student who enjoys turning messy data into clear analysis, practical models, and visual explanations that people can actually use.

About Me

My academic interests sit at the intersection of data science, analytics engineering, and financial technology. I like projects where the final result is not just a number on a screen, but a tool, dashboard, or workflow that helps someone make a better decision. That is one reason I enjoy visualization so much: a well-designed chart can reveal patterns much faster than a long report. I am especially interested in using data to understand changing systems, whether that means market behavior, user activity, or operational performance. Over the last few semesters, I have worked on projects involving Python, SQL, R, machine learning, and web-based data products. Those experiences taught me that good technical work depends on two skills at once: careful implementation and thoughtful communication. A pipeline that works but is impossible to explain is not very helpful, and a polished interface without reliable logic underneath is not enough either. I try to build with both in mind. Outside of class, I enjoy exploring new tools, refining portfolio projects, and learning how to make technical results more accessible through storytelling, interface design, and documentation. This website is a small summary of that approach and the kinds of problems I want to keep solving.

What I Focus On

The projects that motivate me most usually involve a mix of structured data, exploratory analysis, and a final product that others can interact with. I am drawn to work that begins with raw records and ends with something decision-ready, such as a cleaned dataset, a forecasting workflow, a strategy analysis, or a dashboard that helps users spot trends quickly. My coursework and project work have pushed me to think about data not only as an academic subject but also as a practical craft. I care about reproducible analysis, readable code, and interfaces that reduce confusion instead of adding more noise.

Current toolkit

Python, SQL, R, Tableau, Power BI, Git/GitHub, and D3 for interactive visual communication.
