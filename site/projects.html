Selected Work

Projects and interests that shape how I build.

One of the strongest themes across my projects is the idea that analysis becomes more valuable when it can be reused, compared, and communicated clearly. For example, in stock visualization and strategy work, I am interested in building workflows that go beyond a single chart. I want the pipeline to clean data carefully, generate meaningful features, test strategy behavior under different conditions, and present the outcome in a form that makes tradeoffs easy to understand. That same mindset also appears in team projects like PetHub and Tummi, where the task is not just to produce a result, but to connect data, application logic, and user-facing views in a way that feels organized and dependable. Working in teams has also taught me how important it is to build software that others can read and extend. Good collaboration depends on naming things clearly, documenting assumptions, and keeping the structure simple enough that new contributors can move quickly. Going forward, I want to keep building projects that combine analytics, product thinking, and visual storytelling, especially in spaces where users need both precision and clarity.

Topics I want to keep exploring

  • Interactive visualizations that help users compare categories and drill into exact values.
  • Financial analytics workflows that combine cleaning, modeling, and explainable evaluation.
  • Data products that connect databases, APIs, and dashboards into one useful experience.
  • Interface design choices that make technical work feel more understandable and less intimidating.
